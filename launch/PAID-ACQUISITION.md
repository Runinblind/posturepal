# Paid Acquisition Strategy - PosturePal Pro

**Recommended Start:** After organic validation (100+ downloads, 2%+ conversion rate)

**Primary Channel:** Apple Search Ads  
**Secondary:** Meta Ads (Instagram/Facebook)  
**Future:** Google UAC (if Android version)

---

## 🍎 Apple Search Ads - Primary Paid Channel

### Why Apple Search Ads First?

**Pros:**
- High intent users (actively searching App Store)
- Best-performing channel for app downloads
- Tightly integrated with App Store
- Lower CPA than most channels ($1-5 typical for niche apps)
- Easy attribution

**Cons:**
- Limited to iOS users
- Auction-based (costs can spike)
- Requires App Store presence (can't start pre-launch)

---

### Apple Search Ads Campaign Structure

#### Campaign 1: Brand Defense

**Goal:** Capture users searching for "PosturePal" (prevent competitors from stealing traffic)

**Settings:**
- Campaign name: Brand - PosturePal
- Countries: US (expand later)
- Budget: $5-10/day
- Max CPT (cost per tap): $2.00

**Ad Group 1: Exact Brand Match**
- Keywords:
  - posturepal (Exact Match)
  - posture pal (Exact Match)
  - posturepal app (Exact Match)
- Bid: $1.50

**Why This Matters:**
- Competitors can bid on your brand name
- Protect your traffic
- Low cost (usually <$0.50 per tap for own brand)

---

#### Campaign 2: Discovery - Broad Match

**Goal:** Find new keyword opportunities automatically

**Settings:**
- Campaign name: Discovery - Broad
- Budget: $20/day (start low, scale up)
- Max CPT: $2.00
- Search Match: ON (let Apple find relevant keywords)

**Initial Seed Keywords (Broad Match):**
- posture app
- posture reminder
- apple watch health
- desk ergonomics
- back pain relief

**Strategy:**
- Let run for 7 days
- Review Search Terms report
- Add high-performers as Exact Match in Campaign 3
- Add negatives for irrelevant terms

---

#### Campaign 3: Performance - Exact Match

**Goal:** Scale proven keywords efficiently

**Settings:**
- Campaign name: Performance - Exact
- Budget: $30-50/day (or more as CAC allows)
- Max CPT: $3.00

**Ad Groups by Theme:**

**AG 1: Direct Intent**
- posture reminder app (Exact)
- posture tracking app (Exact)
- posture app apple watch (Exact)
- apple watch posture (Exact)

**AG 2: Problem-Aware**
- fix posture app (Exact)
- improve posture app (Exact)
- better posture app (Exact)
- slouching reminder (Exact)

**AG 3: Competitor**
- [Competitor Name] (Exact)
- [Competitor App] alternative (Exact)
- Note: Check App Store policies on competitor bidding

**Bid Strategy:**
- Start with recommended bid
- Increase by 10% if below Top 5 placement
- Decrease if CPA too high

### Target Metrics (Apple Search Ads)

| Metric | Target | Good | Excellent |
|--------|--------|------|-----------|
| TTR (Tap-Through Rate) | 5% | 8% | 12%+ |
| CR (Conversion Rate) | 30% | 40% | 50%+ |
| CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) | <$5 | <$3 | <$2 |
| ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) | 2x | 3x | 5x+ |

**ROAS Calculation:**
- LTV of user (annual sub) = $29.99
- CPA target = $10 or less
- ROAS = $29.99 ÷ $10 = 3x (good)

## 📱 Meta Ads (Instagram/Facebook) - Secondary Channel

### Why Meta Ads?

**Pros:**
- Visual platform (great for lifestyle/health content)
- Precise targeting (interests, behaviors)
- Retargeting capabilities
- Large scale potential

**Cons:**
- iOS 14.5+ tracking limitations (harder attribution)
- Requires strong creative (video > static)
- Learning curve (need to test creative constantly)
- Higher CPA than Apple Search Ads typically

---

**Goal:** Introduce PosturePal to cold audience, build awareness

**Objective:** Video Views

### Creative Best Practices for Meta Ads

**Video Ads (Best Performing):**

**Format:**
- 9:16 vertical (Stories, Reels)
- 1:1 square (Feed)
- 15-30 seconds max

**Structure:**
- Hook (3 seconds): "This is what 8 hours at a desk does to your spine..."
- Problem (5 seconds): Show slouching, pain
- Solution (10 seconds): Introduce PosturePal, show notifications
- Social Proof (5 seconds): User testimonials or stats
- CTA (5 seconds): "Download free today"

**Examples:**
1. **Before/After Split Screen**
   - Left: Person slouching, looking tired
   - Right: Same person, good posture, energized
   - Text overlay: "30 days with PosturePal"

2. **POV Style**
   - First-person view of desk setup
   - Apple Watch notification appears
   - "Posture check! Sit up straight"
   - User adjusts, smiles
   - Text: "Your pocket posture coach"

3. **User Testimonial**
   - Real user (or actor) talking to camera
   - "I used to have chronic back pain from my desk job..."
   - Show app on Apple Watch
   - "PosturePal reminds me every 30 minutes. Game changer."

**Static Image Ads:**
- High-quality screenshot with benefit callouts
- Before/after illustration
- Infographic style (data/stats)
- Less effective than video, but cheaper to produce

## 📊 Tracking & Attribution

### Essential Setup

**1. Apple Search Ads Attribution:**
- Built-in to App Store Connect
- Automatic tracking
- Review in App Analytics > Sources

**2. Meta Ads (Post-iOS 14.5):**
- Use Meta's Aggregated Event Measurement
- Set up App Events in Facebook Events Manager
- Priority events:
  1. Install (highest priority)
  2. First Open
  3. Purchase (Pro upgrade)
  4. Complete Registration

**3. UTM Parameters:**
- Tag all external links
- Example: `https://apps.apple.com/app/posturepal?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=launch`
- Track in Google Analytics

**4. RevenueCat Attribution:**
- RevenueCat can track which ad source drove a subscription
- Essential for calculating true ROAS

## 🚨 Common Paid Acquisition Mistakes

**Don't:**
1. **Start paid ads before organic validation**
   - Fix retention first
   - Ensure 2%+ free → paid conversion
   - Otherwise you're burning money

2. **Set and forget campaigns**
   - Check daily (at least first 2 weeks)
   - Optimize constantly

3. **Run too many tests at once**
   - Change one variable at a time
   - Need statistical significance

4. **Ignore negative keywords**
   - "Free posture app" = low intent
   - "Posture corrector" = might want physical product, not app

5. **Scale too fast**
   - Increase budget 20% at a time
   - Let algorithm re-learn

6. **Use only one creative**
   - Ad fatigue is real
   - Need 3-5 creative variations

7. **Forget about LTV**
   - CPA means nothing without LTV context
   - $20 CPA is fine if LTV is $100

## 🎯 When to Scale Paid Ads

**Scale UP when:**
- CPA < $15 (or your target)
- ROAS > 2x
- Conversion rate stable (not declining as you scale)
- Budget not capped (spending full daily budget)

**Pause or Scale DOWN when:**
- CPA > $30
- ROAS < 1x
- Conversion rate dropping
- High churn (users subscribing then immediately canceling)

## 🔮 Advanced Tactics (Month 3+)

### 1. Seasonal Bidding
- Increase bids during high-intent periods:
  - New Year (resolution season)
  - Back to school (students setting up desks)
  - Black Friday (discount offers)

### 2. Geo-Targeting
- Start US-only
- Expand to English-speaking countries (UK, Canada, Australia)
- Later: Localize for other markets

### 3. Dayparting (Meta)
- Pause ads 12AM-6AM (low intent)
- Increase budget 9AM-12PM, 6PM-9PM (peak browsing)

### 4. Lookalike Audiences (Meta)
- 1% LAL of paid users (highest quality)
- 1% LAL of 7-day retained users
- 1-3% LAL for broader reach

### 5. Cross-Platform Retargeting
- User sees Meta ad → visits App Store → doesn't download
- Retarget with Apple Search Ads (brand keyword)

## 📈 Projected Performance (Month 1)

**Scenario: $1,000 Ad Spend**

**Apple Search Ads ($600):**
- CPA: $3
- Installs: 200
- Free → Paid (3%): 6 paid users
- Revenue (monthly): $29.94
- Revenue (annual subs, $29.99): $0 (assume monthly first)
- Short-term ROAS: 0.05x (loss)
- **But:** LTV over 12 months = $59.88/user × 6 = $359.28
- **Long-term ROAS:** 0.6x (still building)

**Meta Ads ($300):**
- CPA: $15
- Installs: 20
- Free → Paid (2%): 0-1 paid users
- Lower immediate ROI, but brand awareness value

**Total:**
- Spend: $1,000
- Installs: 220
- Paid users: 6-7
- Month 1 Revenue: ~$35
- **Payback period:** ~12-18 months (typical for subscription apps)

**This is normal.** Paid acquisition is a long-term investment. Most successful apps run paid ads at a loss initially, banking on LTV over time.

## 💡 Final Paid Ads Philosophy

**Paid ads are accelerant, not rescue.**

- If organic isn't working, paid won't save you
- Fix product, retention, conversion FIRST
- Then use paid to pour gas on the fire

**Start small, learn fast, scale what works.**
